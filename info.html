Digital Humanities 2013 Information

Date Published: 2013

In order to maintain access to as many resources as possible, the Center for Digital Research in the Humanities, in cooperation with the University of Nebraska–Lincoln Libraries, has created an easier-to-maintain, archived version of this website, while preserving the original content. Every effort has been made to retain URLs where possible. This information is provided “as is” and any updates will appear on this page only.

Changes

This static version of the site has been created using a web crawler. Few changes should be apparent in content and design, but the site no longer depends on the dynamic generation of search results, data, or HTML pages. This reduces server resource demands and overhead for maintenance of the site, which helps us keep older sites available longer. We have made every effort to keep existing URLs in place, and to add forwards from older URLs when we could not. Where possible, we added a JavaScript search page, but only if the original site already had a searching mechanism.

Contact

If you have any questions about the technical aspects of this site or would like to report a problem, please email cdrh@unl.edu.

Data

The data for this site is available on Github and can be downloaded as a zip.

Links

  • Alliance of Digital Humanities Organizations

    The Alliance of Digital Humanities Organizations (ADHO) promotes and supports digital research and teaching across all arts and humanities disciplines, acting as a community-based advisory force, and supporting excellence in research, publication, collaboration and training.

  • The Center for Digital Research in the Humanities

    The Center for Digital Research in the Humanities (CDRH) advances interdisciplinary, collaborative research, and offers forums, workshops and research fellowships for faculty and students in the area of digital scholarship.

  • University of Nebraska–Lincoln

    The University of Nebraska–Lincoln, chartered in 1869, is an educational institution of international stature. UNL is listed by the Carnegie Foundation within the “Research Universities (very high research activity)” category. UNL is a land-grant university and a member of the Association of Public and Land-grant Universities (APLU).
