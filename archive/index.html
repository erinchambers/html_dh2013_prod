Guidelines for Presenters and Session Chairs

ADHO provides guidelines for presenters and for those chairing sessions at Digital Humanities conferences. Further guidance can be found under “Questions and Answers,” below. Please contact us with any additional questions.

Poster Guidelines

Poster size limit is 30″x40″ (76 x 101 centimeters).

If you need to print your poster in Lincoln, UNL has printing at CEHS Design Center. Please contact them directly for printing options and costs.

Questions and Answers

Q: Is there a contributor rate?
A: The rate for contributors is the same as for attendees.

Q: How much time do I have for a short paper? A long paper?
A: Short papers are kept to a strict 10-minute time limit, with questions for all presenters held to the end of the session. Long papers should be presented in 15-20 minutes, saving 5-10 minutes for questions at the end of each paper.

Q: Is session-hopping permitted?
A: Yes. Session chairs will keep each short and long paper strictly to time, so that it is possible for attendees to predict when papers listed in the program will be presented.

Links

  • Alliance of Digital Humanities Organizations

    The Alliance of Digital Humanities Organizations (ADHO) promotes and supports digital research and teaching across all arts and humanities disciplines, acting as a community-based advisory force, and supporting excellence in research, publication, collaboration and training.

  • The Center for Digital Research in the Humanities

    The Center for Digital Research in the Humanities (CDRH) advances interdisciplinary, collaborative research, and offers forums, workshops and research fellowships for faculty and students in the area of digital scholarship.

  • University of Nebraska–Lincoln

    The University of Nebraska–Lincoln, chartered in 1869, is an educational institution of international stature. UNL is listed by the Carnegie Foundation within the “Research Universities (very high research activity)” category. UNL is a land-grant university and a member of the Association of Public and Land-grant Universities (APLU).
